# Executive Dashboard Story — Retail Business Performance 2024–2025

## Executive Summary

The retail business generated $217M in total sales and $33.3M in profit across 2024 and 2025, reflecting a 15.3% blended profit margin. Revenue has been consistent year-on-year, with no dramatic growth trajectory but also no signs of structural decline. The business is profitable, operationally stable, and concentrated heavily in Technology — which creates both strength and vulnerability.

Three themes define the current state of the business: Technology is the growth engine that requires protection. Furniture is a drag on profitability that requires intervention. Discounting is eroding margins in ways that are not fully visible at the aggregate level.

---

## What Is Performing Well

**Technology is the clear profit driver.** With $153.9M in sales and an 18.2% margin, Technology accounts for over 84% of company profit. Sub-categories like Copiers, Accessories, and Phones are consistently high-margin performers. The business has built a strong position here, and this is where investment in sales capacity and customer retention will yield the highest return.

**The South region is the strongest market.** It leads in both revenue ($64.7M) and absolute profit ($10M). While margins are broadly similar across regions, the South's ability to generate higher volume suggests stronger market penetration or more effective sales coverage than other regions.

**Home Office is the most profitable customer segment.** At $74.5M in sales and $11.6M in profit (15.5% margin), Home Office edges out Corporate and Consumer. This is notable given that Home Office customers typically place smaller but more frequent orders. The segment shows potential for further growth if targeted with the right product mix.

**The second half of 2025 showed meaningful momentum.** July and August 2025 were the two strongest months on record, reaching $10.7M and $10.9M respectively. This may signal that marketing or operational improvements made earlier in the year are starting to bear fruit.

---

## What Is Underperforming

**Furniture is struggling on every meaningful metric.** A 6.9% profit margin is thin for a category that generates over $51M in sales. When combined with a 7.7% return rate — the highest of any category — the actual profitability of Furniture is likely worse than headline numbers suggest. Tables and Bookcases are the worst performers, sitting at 5.7% margin each.

**Mid-2024 saw a concerning sales dip.** June through August 2024 saw sales fall below $8M per month, with August reaching a low of $6.3M. The business recovered in September, but the dip points to either seasonal softness or a period of reduced commercial activity that needs to be understood before it repeats.

**Standard Class shipping is generating friction.** With an average delivery time of 4.7 days, Standard Class is slow relative to alternatives. Approximately 16% of all orders take 6 or more days to deliver, which likely affects customer satisfaction and may be contributing to returns.

---

## Risks Visible in the Data

**Discounting is quietly destroying margin.** Orders with discounts above 20% generate average profit of under $3,000 — less than a quarter of what low-discount orders produce. Critically, this pattern is not visible in top-line sales numbers, which makes it easy for leadership to underestimate the impact. The business needs a discount governance framework.

**Technology concentration is a strategic vulnerability.** Over 70% of sales and 84% of profit come from a single category. A shift in technology pricing, a supply disruption, or a change in competitive dynamics could have a disproportionate impact on the business. The other categories are not yet positioned to compensate.

**Furniture returns are a margin risk that compounds over time.** At 7.7% return rate and 6.9% margin, Furniture transactions that result in returns are almost certainly unprofitable on a fully-loaded cost basis. If return rates are trending upward in this category, the situation will worsen.

---

## Growth Opportunities

**Organic and Email channels are the most efficient marketing investments.** Organic channel drives $88.8M in sales — more than double any paid channel — with a 15.1% margin. Email is the second most efficient paid channel at 15.5% margin on $34.5M in sales. These are the channels worth scaling.

**The West region has room to grow.** Sales in the West are lower than the South and North despite being a large and commercially active market. If the business can bring West region performance closer to South region levels, the incremental revenue opportunity is meaningful.

**Same Day and First Class shipping could become a differentiator.** These modes achieve delivery in under 2 days on average. Promoting fast shipping as a premium feature — particularly for Technology products — could improve conversion and customer satisfaction scores without significant cost impact if the uptake remains targeted.

---

## Recommended Business Actions

1. **Cap discount authority at 20%** for front-line sales without manager approval. Review all current accounts where discounts routinely exceed this threshold.

2. **Conduct a Furniture category review** covering product margins, return root causes, and pricing strategy. Determine whether Tables and Bookcases should be repriced, repositioned, or phased out.

3. **Build a South region playbook** that documents what is working — coverage model, channel mix, promotional cadence — and pilot it in the West region in the next planning cycle.

4. **Introduce a delivery experience initiative** targeting the 16% of orders taking 6+ days. Start with proactive communication and explore whether Standard Class logistics partners can improve their SLA.

5. **Protect Technology margin** by monitoring sub-category margin trends quarterly. Accessories and Phones are high-volume and high-margin — any margin erosion here would have an outsized impact on company profitability.

---

## Limitations of This Dashboard

- The dashboard reflects two years of transactional data (2024–2025) and cannot speak to longer-term trends or pre-2024 performance.
- Customer acquisition costs and marketing spend data are not available, so ROI by campaign channel cannot be fully calculated.
- The data does not include competitor pricing or market share, so regional performance gaps cannot be fully explained by internal factors alone.
- Return reason codes are not available, making it difficult to distinguish quality issues from customer behavior issues.
- Customer-level retention or repeat purchase data is not modeled here. The segment-level analysis treats all orders equally regardless of whether they come from new or returning customers.

---

## Suggested Next Analysis

- **Customer cohort analysis:** Understand repeat purchase behavior and identify which segment or channel produces the most loyal customers.
- **Discount elasticity study:** Model whether reducing discounts from the 25–35% band would result in lost volume or simply improved margin on the same orders.
- **Furniture root cause analysis:** Break down Furniture returns by sub-category, ship mode, and region to isolate the key drivers.
- **Predictive sales forecasting:** Use the two-year monthly trend to build a rolling 3-month sales forecast that leadership can track against actuals.
